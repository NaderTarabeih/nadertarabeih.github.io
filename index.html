My name is Dr. Nader Tarabeih, and I am writing to propose a collaboration and to seek support for my ongoing postdoctoral research fellowship. I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Adelson School of Medicine, Ariel University, Israel, under the supervision of Professor Gregory (Zvi) Livshits. My research focuses on the interplay between inflammatory biomarkers, body composition measurements, genetic factors, musculoskeletal disorders, and metabolic comorbidities, including type 2 diabetes, hyperlipidemia, and cardiovascular disease.

I hold a Ph.D. in Anatomy and Anthropology from the Sackler Faculty of Medicine, Tel Aviv University, where I established a comprehensive family-based clinical cohort comprising over 1,000 individuals from 98 extended Arab families. This cohort includes extensive clinical assessments, detailed lumbar and cervical pain questionnaires, blood samples, whole-spine imaging (CT and MRI scans), biochemical profiling (cytokines, growth factors, pro- and anti-inflammatory markers), anthropometric and body composition measurements (fat mass, skeletal muscle mass, total body water, among others), and fasting blood samples for complete blood counts and (mainly white and red blood cells) and blood chemistry including high-sensitivity CRP (hs-CRP) levels, glucose, and lipid profiles (triglycerides, total cholesterol, HDL-C, LDL-C). All biological samples have been biobanked in aliquots and stored at -80C, ensuring readiness for advanced genetic, metabolomic, or epigenetic analyses.

Through this project, I gained significant expertise in clinical cohort management, ELISA techniques, body composition analysis, genetic epidemiology, and biomarker discovery. My work has resulted in 12 peer-reviewed publications in high-impact Q1 and Q2 journals (please see the attached publication list), with over 100 citations to date.

Additionally, we have recently completed a major cross-sectional study focusing on sarcopenic obesity in a cohort of 562 women, analyzing body composition, inflammatory biomarkers, and metabolic profiles. We are currently preparing this manuscript for submission.

In my current postdoctoral research, I am further exploring the contribution of specific inflammatory factors and genetic pleiotropic mechanisms to the development of musculoskeletal and metabolic disorders. I am particularly interested in integrating biomarker profiling, body composition parameters, and genetic epidemiological modeling to develop new predictive frameworks for chronic disease risk stratification.

I am actively seeking collaboration opportunities with leading research groups and support for my postdoctoral project, including access to complementary expertise, advanced technological platforms, and potential funding mechanisms. I firmly believe that through interdisciplinary collaboration, we can significantly advance the understanding of chronic disease mechanisms and contribute to the development of better preventive and therapeutic strategies.

I would be delighted to discuss potential areas of collaboration in more detail. Thank you very much for considering my proposal. I look forward to the opportunity to work together.
